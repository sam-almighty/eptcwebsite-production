  • Family Benefit Fund

    The EPTC will strive hard to do its best and introduce the benefits for the betterment of the members. Please pay your subscription regularly to avoid becoming defaulter. And loose the benefits extended to you all. One of the Community development projects evolved during 125th anniversary of our church celebration, to help the poor and needy of all denominations Family benefit fund: This fund was started only to help the congregation members during their crisis and hard days. A separate subcommittee is formed from the pastorate committee and members elected in the general body, under the chairman, every biennium to monitor this function and funds. This was one of the 4 programmes we started during 125th year anniversary celebration under the Community development programme,.

    Congregation family contributes Rs 50/- towards this fund and a separate account is maintained and operated.

    The following are the benefits extended to the families.

    1. In the event of death, the relied admissible is Rs 15000/- in cash to nominee.
    2. Financial benefits shall be extended to the registered members who are diagnosed/treated for terminal diseases like cancer, renal failure, AIDS or major Heart/Brain surgery/kidney Transplantation.
    3. Rs 5000/- being paid per cataract surgery. On producing the Hospital Bills
    4. Rs 1000/- being paid for optical lens on production of such bills/payments.

  • Tailoring Class

    This course is designed to teach the various nuances of pattern making and fashion dress designing in a comprehensive manner. The patterns cater to both standard measurements & personal. By learning different types of patterns, a student would be able to stylishly design her own dress according to the latest trends. All the necessary and important dresses for everyday use are taught. Pursuing this course will enable a person to understand the different aspects of pattern making and tailoring in a shorter time frame. He student learns about measurements of a specific garment and the way to measure a person for the same. Guidance on Instructions and calculations for drafting and then Drafting of the pattern on the paper and then on fabric and the method to cut and stitch the fabric without lining and with lining would be taught. One of the Community development projects evolved during 125th anniversary of our church celebration, to help the poor and needy of all denominations.

  • Choir Group

    The Choir is a well-organized group of like minded congregation members, which provides a variety of high-quality choral music, and provides an opportunity for singers to enhance their musical and singing skills. The main function of the church choir is to celebrate Sunday worship through Hymns & Lyrics with Choral and Mass settings. A unique aspect of the choir is that from time to time it presents major choral works of composers of classical era and participating in events organized by churches and Institutions of KCD. The Choir today is comprised of approximately twenty five volunteer singers who rehearse together weekly on Saturdays 6.30-9.00 PM.

  • Bajana

    It is a tradition in Tamil speaking churches that Bajana an Indian style of singing and praising and proclaiming the birth of Jesus Christ By songs visiting the congregation member’s houses, Every Year the Pastorate committee elects one person as convenor of this Bajana, under the able guidance of the Chairman. His year that is for the biennium 2015-2017, Mr. Selwyn Jebasingh is elected from the PC as Bajana convenor.

    This year the Bajana singing has commenced from 1st December and end with 17th Dec 15, covering all the 370 congregation members.

    The programme and the land mark to visit the houses will be prepared well in advance, area wise by the secretary along with the chairman.

    I would also like to mention that every day one of the congregation member welcome them with dinner at the closure of that day singing. From the Pastor's house

    The returns of the offering received will be spent for the Poor and the needy during Christmas season irrespective of caste colour and creed.
